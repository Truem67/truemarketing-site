Day 1
The CCMA doesn't care what you remember.
Send immediately on Day 1 · Pain hook

Hi [First Name],

When an employee takes you to the CCMA, you will sit in front of a commissioner and explain what happened. You will remember the verbal warning. You will remember the performance conversation. You will remember agreeing on a deadline.

The commissioner will ask for the documentation.

If it does not exist — or if it only exists as an email that the employee says they never received — your memory does not count for much.

The CCMA does not rule on intent. It rules on evidence. And in most unfair dismissal disputes, the employer loses not because they did something wrong, but because they cannot prove they did something right.

Tomorrow I will show you a way to create documentation that cannot be disputed, deleted, or backdated.
